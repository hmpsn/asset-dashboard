# UI Rebuild Kit — Persona + Craft Design-Review Audit (Synthesis)

> **ADVISORY ONLY. No code was changed. Every finding here is input to an owner decision — the owner decides what (if anything) gets built or amended.**
>
> Scope: the proposed hmpsn.studio platform rebuild — the UI Rebuild Kit at `hmpsn studio Design System/` (hi-fi prototype: `mockup/*.js` + `Keywords & Flows Mockup.html`, `Client Dashboard Mockup.html`, Handoff Brief, conventions docs).
> Question under review: **is this the right build for a solo-founder SEO/web-analytics agency, and is its UI/UX sound for admins and clients?**
>
> Inputs synthesized: 9 recovered panel personas + 6 craft/strategy reviews (`docs/ui-rebuild/phase0/checkpoints/wf2-design-review-recovered.json`), 1 new persona (multi-location/franchise operator), 35 independent validations (all cited findings below marked CONFIRMED were verified against the actual mockup/font/doc files), and the neutral digest + craft docs at `docs/ui-rebuild/phase0/design-review/`.

---

## 1. HEADLINE

**Verdict on "is this the right build?": the thesis is right; the prototype-as-spec is not yet safe to fan out from. Direction: yes. Current state: conditional — resolve a short list of structural decisions before any build code.**

All 10 personas returned **builtToMySpec: "partially"** — zero "no" verdicts, zero full "yes" verdicts. The organizing concepts (VERDICT→VALUE→PROOF trust spine, provenance-labelled money, graduation rule, cross-book Command Center, send boundary, dual-register recs) are validated wins that multiple personas explicitly co-signed and the benchmark review rates categorically ahead of AgencyAnalytics/Semrush-class tooling. The kit is the first design several personas said "actually understands my job."

Two structural findings matter above everything else:

1. **The kit ships two competing, unresolved client-facing designs — and the one that reads as "the client experience" (portal.js) hardwires the exact pattern clients fire agencies over.** `portal.js:382-388` structurally templates the verdict on the **traffic delta** ("You're climbing — traffic's up 34% this quarter") — this is the rule, not placeholder copy — with an impressions-first PROOF section and a client-facing 94/100 invented health-score ring (`portal.js:249`). Four-plus personas (marketing lead, HVAC owner, B2B SaaS lead, VC/board) independently flagged this; all CONFIRMED. Meanwhile the *other* client design (Client Dashboard Mockup) has the right bones — leads-anchored verdict, retainer-relative ROI, named leads, one-pager export — but is 1 tab built of 5. Indispensable features are split across the two designs (export + named leads only in the dashboard; approve/reply loop only in the portal), the Handoff Brief's Phase-0 gate leaves the choice open, and a third owner-RATIFIED client IA (the Issue four-tab shell) already exists at HEAD. Picking wrong, or letting a build agent pick implicitly, is the single largest risk in the kit.

2. **The business itself is missing from the design.** Client-side monetization (TierGate, 14-day trial, Plans/ROI, cart-checkout, per-item purchase, upgrade moments) has **no designed home anywhere in the kit** (CONFIRMED by grep across all mockups + parity ledger + permissions matrix), despite being the revenue model and existing shipped capability — a violation of the kit's own additive-only mandate. The same absence pattern repeats for the notification spine (no admin job/notification bell — a regression vs HEAD's NotificationBell on a heavily async platform, CONFIRMED — and zero client push/alert design, which four client personas independently named as their actual return-visit mechanism), and for the ratified navigation model (the two-zone rail's BOOK zone is literally `const book = []` — Command Center reachable only by clicking the logo, CONFIRMED).

A third pre-build architectural call was surfaced by the new panel seat: **location is not a data dimension anywhere in the kit** — the entire scope model is one-client-one-workspace-one-story (`workspace.js:1-16`, CONFIRMED). The multi-location operator's blocker stands on its own merits and is cheap to decide now, expensive to retrofit.

**Bottom line:** protect the trust spine, the provenance ladder, and the Command Center — they are genuinely differentiating and the panel wants them. But "the prototype is the spec" is currently false advertising: the spec contains dead primary buttons, phantom handoffs, a verdict template that inverts the platform's own retention thesis, an unimplemented ratified nav, and no monetization. Close the pre-build list below first.

The pattern: the provenance/honesty layer has landed; the *compulsion* layer (leads-anchored verdicts, CAC/payback framing, push-driven cadence, reconcilable numbers) is what converts trust into retention and expansion — and it is exactly the layer the launch-blockers sit on.

## 4. WHAT IS GOOD — validated strengths to protect through the rebuild

These were independently praised by craft reviews AND co-signed by personas; several are ahead of the commercial category. Protect them as named parity-ledger rows so build agents cannot simplify them away.

1. **The VERDICT→VALUE→PROOF trust spine as the platform's organizing law** — every client narrative opens with a plain-English verdict, then the dollar band, then proof; the plan/work-log demoted below proof. The exact inversion seven personas demanded in the prior round; benchmark review rates it "categorically ahead of AgencyAnalytics/Looker-style read-only dashboards." (digest §3.1; portal + CDM + Insights Engine.)
2. **The provenance ladder** — estimate→measured→actual chips, 'agency estimate' basis badges, methodology arithmetic spelled out, and the honest dash when lead tracking isn't wired ("connects once your form is wired up") instead of a fabricated count. "A trust feature no mainstream SEO tool ships" (benchmark), independently praised by churned client, multi-location op, VC. The posture problem (B11) is about *which rung leads*, not the ladder itself.
3. **The cross-book Command Center / verdict-first operator home** — one unified work queue (Optimizations / To send / Monetization) replacing 9 stat cards + four triage systems; "Linear-grade triage applied to SEO ops — neither Ahrefs, Semrush, nor AgencyAnalytics has any cross-project work queue" (benchmark). The solo founder called it "the first design that actually understands my job." It just needs to be in the rail (B4).
4. **The send boundary + dual-register Recommendations** — dark operator workbench above, light curated client surface below; every rec re-authored in client language with an in-place "What Acme sees" preview. The core idea is right and unique; flows craft explicitly marks the dual-pane curation pattern PROTECT.
5. **The pipeline lifecycle + decay loop** — intake→brief→draft→review→published→graduate, with Content Health re-entering decayed pages into the same lifecycle; the Published mode framed as "results, not a to-do"; factual-accuracy/hallucination gates route-to-human by design (the copy is right — the state machine behind it is B-list work).
6. **The graduation rule** — technical wins stay in the workbench and only graduate to the client when they become a provable story. Structurally the anti-noise mechanism every client persona asked for.
7. **The client-portal voice** — "Found in Google", "was #10 · now #4", consequence-framed fixes ("shoppers were hitting dead ends"), the honest degraded state, the accountable declining-client lede. Content craft: "the best client copy in the project's history." (The traffic-led H1 template it sits under is B2 — fix the frame, keep the voice.)
8. **Token architecture and theming discipline** — engineered light theme (darkened accents computed to 5.0–5.5:1), tokens-only styling, global keyboard-only focus ring, reduced-motion kill switch. Worth building on once the specific contrast/z-index/tnum defects are patched.
9. **Admin IA reasoning artifacts** — the scope×audience grid, Navigation Model Options, cold-start-as-onboarding ("a new client's cockpit isn't a broken dashboard — it's the setup flow"), command-palette parity with HEAD. IA craft calls these "exemplary IA reasoning worth protecting."
10. **The two-way client loop in embryo** — portal replies → operator inbox/cockpit, promotable requests → strategy signals, client-declined keywords *with stated reasons* feeding back into Keywords. Personas want it governed (confirms, records), not removed.

## 5. RECOMMENDATIONS — ranked

**[pre-build] = must be decided/fixed before any build fan-out. [during-build] = contract to enforce as surfaces are built. [fast-follow] = ship after the core, explicitly scheduled.**

1. **[pre-build] Resolve the client-design fork — the kit's own Phase-0 gate — with a decided synthesis, not a coin flip.** Default per the kit's own Client Surface Sweep: finish the owner-ratified Issue four-tab cutover as the shell; adopt the Client Dashboard Mockup's bones (leads-anchored verdict, retainer-relative ROI ledger, named leads, one-pager export, under-the-hood collapse) as the Overview; port the portal's approve/reply/greenlight loop and its voice. Kill the traffic-led verdict template outright: the H1 anchors on outcomes (leads/actions vs baseline), with symmetric honesty — the down-branch states its number the same way the up-branch does. Do not close the gate on Overview alone: require Inbox (the action loop + trust guards) and at least one designed pay-more moment first. (Resolves B1, B2; addresses marketing lead, HVAC, B2B, VC, churned.)
2. **[pre-build] Design the business model into the kit.** Add a tier×trial axis to the Content & Access permissions matrix and the Handoff Brief template; add parity-ledger rows for TierGate, 14-day trial, Plans/ROI, cart-checkout, per-item purchase, upgrade moments; fix the Growth-badge-with-Premium-semantics contradiction explicitly (fix the mockup or deliberately amend MONETIZATION.md — never hand the ambiguity to a build agent); design the Free-tier and trial client states. (B3.)
3. **[pre-build] Decide now: location is a first-class dimension of one client account, not N workspaces.** This is a data-model decision, not a UI commitment — the portfolio UI itself can be a fast-follow, but the scope model (`workspace.js`'s flat client array becoming client→locations) must be decided before the foundation is poured, because the kit has already proven the needed pattern (Command Center's ranked cross-entity triage + book Site Health roll-up) and merely needs it re-pointable at a client's locations. Record the decision against the standing Q7 deferral either way. (B13.)
4. **[pre-build] Fix the shell contracts once, before 18+ surfaces clone them.** (a) Populate the rail's BOOK zone per the ratified Model B (Today, Inbox, Site Health·book, Action Results·book) or formally amend the model — no logo-as-door; (b) add the background-job/notification affordance to the shell spec, wired to existing useBackgroundTasks/NotificationBell semantics; (c) extend the surface map to the true ~28 views with explicit fates for the omitted ten; (d) carry HEAD's nav-registry pattern (one source feeding rail/palette/breadcrumbs + completeness contract test); (e) resolve the AI chat homes (AdminChat, client ChatPanel) as the Phase-0 stop-and-ask the kit itself requires; (f) require route/query params in every surface ticket so `gotoView()`+localStorage is not copied literally. (B4, B6-admin, B10; benchmark + IA majors.)
5. **[pre-build] Patch the primitive-level blockers at the source, once.** (a) Numeral strategy: license a DIN cut with tnum or mandate Inter/JetBrains Mono for all data columns, DIN for headings/hero only, plus a lint rule; (b) purge client-facing purple at source (portal.js recolor, IntentTag 'local' remap, remove purple from Badge's public union) and mirror the platform's client-path purple grep as a gate; (c) fix keyboard access once in the four core primitives (DataTable keyboard rows + table semantics; shared focus-trap/Escape/inert for Drawer/Modal/Popover) and gate the pilot on a keyboard-only walkthrough; (d) one-line law fixes: MetricRing ≥80→emerald, StatusBadge live→emerald, Meter default→blue; (e) token pass: brighten/reclassify `--brand-text-dim`, text-safe light-mode mint, re-point overlay z-indexes at the token scale; (f) write the two missing contracts — hit-target policy and responsive/shell-collapse contract with portal + recommendations declared mobile-required. (B7, B8, B9 + visual majors.)
6. **[during-build] Design the send-client-update flow end-to-end before surface fan-out — it is the product's spine and currently a dead button on six screens.** POV composer, client preview reusing the recs dual-pane, pessimistic confirm, sent-state + history/archive. In the same pass: resolve the three phantom handoffs as state, not toasts (stage-into-Insights creates a move; graduate creates a client story; rec approval creates a pipeline card — the working `sendToPipeline` is the pattern), and make intake dismissal confirmed + undoable + recorded. (B5.)
7. **[during-build] Make mutation and lifecycle discipline a required ticket field.** Publish a mutation classification table (optimistic / optimistic+undo / pessimistic+confirm per action) alongside the Action Registry; make review gates real state-machine transitions (block review→scheduled on failing human-required gates, named-override confirm; add backward transitions), aligned with the repo's existing `server/state-machines.ts` convention; adopt one lifecycle envelope with transition tables for the five new stage vocabularies and run the 'published'/'approved' word-collision pass. (Flows majors; junior SEO's "send buttons that double-check me.")
8. **[during-build] Rebalance the money posture from estimate-led to measured-led.** Lead with measured/actual wherever tracking is connected and make "connect call tracking" the explicit per-client upgrade CTA (the cleanest pay-more lever three personas named); keep estimate heroes banded per their own methodology rule (no '$18,400 · 4.6×' false precision); lock comparison windows; visible restatement note whenever the headline number is re-based; source attribution on measured client metrics ("data from Google Search Console / GA4"); one canonical formatter with the ≥$10k-vs-$8.9k contradiction resolved. (B11, B14-part; churned, dentist, HVAC, multi-location, VC.)
9. **[during-build] Client-copy pass before any client surface builds.** Remove the "curated story, not the workbench" footer from anything client-visible; rewrite client-triggered toasts in client vocabulary with no admin deep-links; add the admin→client term map to Content & Access Conventions inheriting `shared/types/client-vocabulary.ts` and the locked-copy pattern; ratify or revert label drift ('Share with client'→'Send to client'; 'portal' is on the repo's DO-NOT list); demo all four states with real copy on the reference screen so ~54 surface-states are not invented per-agent. (B12, B14; content majors.)
10. **[during-build] Sequence the fan-out revenue-first and name the scope boundary.** Client portal/dashboard, Recommendations (currently a 4-item flat list — the client-facing money surface is one of the thinnest views), and Pipeline land before long-tail admin tools; state in the plan that the rebuild is the *landing surface* while event-driven push, the email preference center, and named-record reconciliation are the actual return/trust loop and stay prioritized independently — the Issue spec's own "do not design for habitual logins" mandate. (Business-fit craft.)

## 6. FAST-FOLLOWS — explicitly deferred, explicitly scheduled

Deferred ≠ dropped. Each of these was requested by named reviewers and should carry a roadmap entry so it is not designed-around a third time.

1. **Client alert/notification spine** — event-driven push + email digest with deep links ("Westside profile suspended, costing ~15 calls/week"); the difference between a weekly open and a monthly invoice-day glance for the dentist, HVAC owner, B2B lead, and multi-location operator. (Depends on rec 4's shell affordance + rec 10's scope naming.)
2. **Portfolio layer for multi-location clients** — portfolio verdict → ranked needs-attention with $ impact → top-3 overperformers with the why → "X of 22 clean" bar → sortable location table drilling into the existing per-location Overview. Reuses Command Center + book Site Health patterns; buildable only if rec 3's data-model call is made now. Includes portfolio-grade one-pager export and cross-location benchmarking.
3. **Named-lead reconciliation** — call/CRM tracking integration graduating "Sarah K. called from the emergency-dentist page" from teaser to system of record; the churned client's and multi-location operator's #1 stay/leave trigger, and the dentist's "make sure your 41 calls survives a conversation with my front desk."
4. **Board/VC framing** — organic CAC next to paid CAC, payback figure, and cohort durability ("are last quarter's pages still earning this quarter?") as an exportable view; converts the VC from "keep funding" to "argue to expand."
5. **Portal memory** — past-updates archive (sent Insights Engine issues are already persisted artifacts) + per-update PDF/export; table stakes for agency reporting and the churned client's "verify past claims" demand. (Or a signed-off decision not to build it.)
6. **Date-range + period-comparison parity with HEAD** — DateRangeSelector named as the primitive in Search & Traffic / Performance / AI Visibility tickets.
7. **Shell-level table interaction contract** — column sort, multi-select + bulk bar, Esc/arrow-key behavior, defined once (the flagship Keywords table currently has none; also the prerequisite for the portfolio table).
8. **Competitive named-rival framing** for the consulting/B2B personas ("us vs the named firms we lose deals to") layered onto the existing competitor data.
9. **HubSpot/GA4-adjacent outbound wiring** (B2B lead: "wire the number to my HubSpot and the summary to my inbox") — downstream of the external-outcome-ingestion track already shipped to staging.
10. **White-label decision log** — "powered by hmpsn studio" is hard-rendered; correct for an owner-operated agency today, but log the decision given it is a core tier feature across the category.
11. **Pipeline matrix/templates mode** — built but unreachable (no mode button); either wire the entry point or cut it from the spec per the dead-control sweep.
