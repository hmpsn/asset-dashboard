# The Issue — Client Discovery + Build-to-Spec (advisory)

**Date:** 2026-06-20  ·  **Method:** clean-room generative discovery — 7 client personas described what THEY want from an SEO agency dashboard from first principles (they were NOT shown the current build), synthesized into one client spec, then diffed against what we actually shipped.  ·  **Personas:** local dentist · busy SaaS marketing lead · VC backer (board audience) · consulting-firm marketing · non-marketing SMB founder (check-signer) · churned ex-agency client · multi-location operator.

> **Advisory only.** No code changed. This is the inverse of the round-2 audit: instead of asking the personas whether our build works, we asked them what they want — then measured the distance. The headline distance is large and structural; see the Gap Analysis verdict.

---

# Part 1 — The Client Spec (built from the personas)

## A. The one-sentence client thesis

**"In sixty seconds, tell me — in my own words and numbers I can check against my own reality — whether the money I send you is producing more of the right customers (or pipeline, or CAC efficiency) than it costs, show me the proof you actually did the work, and tell me the one thing you need from me — otherwise let me close the tab and trust it's handled."**

Every one of the seven personas, unprompted, described the same arc: *one honest verdict → the dollar/lead proof behind it → what's blocked on me → evidence of work → and bury the SEO mechanics*. They differ only in what counts as "a customer" and at what altitude. The product is a **trust instrument first, a reporting tool second** (the churned client's exact framing — and the only one who said it out loud, but all seven implied it).

---

## B. The universal spine — default top-to-bottom layout

This order is not invented; all seven independently dictated it. Where they used different nouns ("new patients," "pipeline," "qualified inquiries," "leads," "new-patient calls across 22 locations"), the *slot* is identical. The adaptive layer (Section C) swaps the noun; the spine never moves.

**1. The one-line verdict (plain English, dated baseline, trend).**
The single sentence a human could say out loud. *Not a chart — a conclusion.*
- Dentist: *"New patient inquiries this month: 14, up from 9 last month."*
- HVAC owner: *"This month SEO drove 23 leads (up from 17)."*
- Churned client: *"Search-driven inquiries up 18% vs. when we started (Jan): 47 vs. 31."*
- Consulting: *"9 qualified inbound inquiries (up from 6), driven mostly by your tax-controversy content."*
- Multi-location: *"Total new-patient leads across 22 locations, this period vs. last."*
- VC: *"Organic CAC this quarter: $X, down Y% vs last Q, vs blended paid CAC of $Z — on track / off track / too early."*
- SaaS lead: *"Organic-sourced + influenced pipeline $X vs last, 4.2x return."*

**Why it leads:** Every persona said the same thing — *"I have 30–90 seconds,"* *"lead with the answer, not the evidence,"* *"if I have to assemble the conclusion myself, you've lost me."* The verdict must be **dated against a baseline** ("vs. when we started") — the HVAC owner and churned client both said a number with no baseline is "just a number I can't judge."

**2. The outcome count, broken into real human units, with trend vs. last period AND vs. baseline.**
Calls + form fills + bookings (local) / leads + demos + qualified conversions (B2B) / qualified inquiries by title & company (services). Clickable to the actual list.
- Demanded by: **all seven.** The churned client and consulting lead specifically want it *clickable to the actual named inquiries* — "if you can show me the actual inquiries with names, I will trust everything else more."

**3. The money frame — outcome value vs. retainer, in dollars.**
*"14 new patients ≈ $11,000 in estimated new production. Your retainer: $1,500."*
- Local trio (dentist, HVAC, churned): rough, **clearly-labeled** dollar estimate. The dentist will accept ~$600–$1,200/patient math; the HVAC owner says *"if you make me do that math myself, I'll do it wrong and in your disfavor."*
- B2B/board (SaaS, VC, consulting): pipeline $ → eventually closed-won, as a **ratio** (4.2x) or **CAC/payback**.
- **Trust guard built in:** the VC's "false precision reads as spin" — never show ROI to two decimals on estimated data. Label estimates as estimates.

**4. What needs ME — the decision/approval queue (max 3, with a clear ask, one button each), or "Nothing needed from you right now."**
- Demanded by: **all seven**, every one calling it out by name. *"Tell me if the ball is in my court"* (dentist). *"My biggest fear is silent stalling on my side"* (SaaS). *"Half the reason these stall is the agency's waiting on me and I don't know it"* (HVAC). The explicit *"Nothing needed"* state matters — it's the permission to close the tab happily.

**5. What YOU did since I last looked + what's shipping next — concise, outcome-tagged, with clickable links to the live thing.**
- Demanded by: **all seven**, but with a sharp split in *why* (see below). Universal rules: scannable not a changelog dump; **outcome-tagged** not a task log (*"published 3 partner-bylined pieces, two now ranking page one"* — not *"fixed 14 broken links"*); **dated and clickable to the live artifact** (the churned client's "trust core").
- **Placement law:** this sits *below* results for every persona. *"Activity is reassurance, not the headline"* (HVAC). *"I never want the work-log to be the first thing I see."*

**6. Everything an SEO cares about but the client doesn't — rankings, traffic, technical health — collapsed below the fold / behind "under the hood."**
- Demanded by: **all seven** as a *place to put the noise*, not delete it. The churned client: *"I'll expand it when I want to audit you, but it must never be the first thing I see."* For local clients this collapses to near-nothing; for the SaaS lead and consulting lead it retains **commercial-intent keyword movement** and **competitor share-of-voice** as a real (if secondary) section.

## C. Segment-adaptive layers

The spine is fixed. Five things adapt: **(1) the outcome noun, (2) the altitude of the money frame, (3) whether competitors/authority surface, (4) whether there's a portfolio roll-up, (5) the export format.**

### Local SMB — single-location service (dentist, HVAC, churned client)
- **Outcome noun:** phone calls + form fills + bookings + direction requests. *"Calls and form fills are real things I understand."*
- **Money frame:** rough dollar estimate per lead × count vs. retainer, in one sentence "I could read aloud to my spouse."
- **The one ranking-ish thing that survives:** the **map pack / "[service] near me"** position and **Google reviews status** (rating, new reviews, unanswered/negative flagged). The dentist: *"the map pack is 90% of how new patients find me… reviews are the thing I can act on myself today."* This is the ONLY local audience for whom reviews are a first-screen citizen — and for them it's near-indispensable.
- **No competitor table, no authority topics, no portfolio.** Single P&L, single front door.
- **Return:** SMS/email push (see F).

### B2B / SaaS — pipeline-led
- **Outcome noun:** qualified leads / demos / conversions → **pipeline $** → **influenced revenue**, reconciled to **HubSpot/GA4**.
- **Money frame:** ROI ratio at top (4.2x); CAC-beats-paid as the renewal argument.
- **Competitors/authority:** YES — **share-of-voice on commercial-intent terms** vs. 2-3 named competitors; "the 10 money terms, not 500."
- **Adds two slots the locals don't get:** (a) **commercial-intent keyword movement** with a so-what, (b) emerging **AI-answer visibility** (ChatGPT/Perplexity) — both the SaaS lead and consulting lead volunteered this as a pay-more frontier.
- **Export:** a **board/QBR-ready one-pager, zero editing** — "save me the rebuild-it-in-slides tax."

### Board / VC audience — efficiency-led
- This persona **never logs in monthly** and barely logs in at all — they *see a screenshot*. So their "first screen" is really **the exported one-pager and the two-line push**, not the live app.
- **Outcome noun:** **organic CAC vs. blended/paid CAC**, payback in months, organic as a **growing share of total pipeline over 4-6 quarters**.
- **The moat slot (unique to VC):** the **compounding-asset view** — "is last quarter's work still earning this quarter with zero new spend?" No other persona asked for this; it's the thesis they underwrote.
- **Hard rules:** honest denominator (retainer + content + internal time visible), **consistent attribution methodology shown** ("the moment the definition of organic-sourced moves to flatter the number, I stop believing all of it"), **no all-green** ("real channels have bad quarters"), **no invented scores with no dollar translation** ("a visibility score of 82 means nothing — I can't put it in a model").

### Professional services — authority-led
- **Outcome noun:** **qualified inbound from the right titles/firms** — "GC-of-a-Fortune-2000, not students and vendors." Quality of inquiry is part of the headline, not a footnote.
- **The authority slot replaces the keyword slot:** "are we page-one for the **15-20 expertise topics partners get hired for**, vs **named competitor firms**" — framed by topic and competitor, never by "position 1-100."
- **Two unique pay-mores:** **brand/reputation mention monitoring** (being cited in the right outlets = how they get hired) and **AI-answer visibility** for "who's the best firm for X."
- **A unique trust guard — credibility is sacred:** *"I'd churn over a single embarrassing piece published under a partner's name."* The work-log section must let them **see/approve partner-bylined content before it ships** — this fuses spine slots 4 and 5 for this segment.
- **Export:** partner-ready monthly summary, forwardable unedited.

### Multi-location — portfolio/triage-led
- **The spine inverts into a roll-up + triage view.** The verdict (slot 1) becomes a **portfolio number** (total leads across all locations + cost-per-lead/revenue trend).
- **Slot 2 becomes the product itself:** a **ranked "needs attention" list** — the 3-5 locations slipping most, each with a plain-English reason and lead/$ impact (*"Westside: calls down 30% MoM, Google profile suspended 11 days ago"*), **biggest-impact first.** *"This IS the product to me. My job is triage."*
- **A second triage slot the others don't have:** **top 3 over-performers + why** ("multi-location is a copy-paste game — tell me why Northgate is crushing it so I can force the playbook on the other 21") and a **consistency roll-up** ("X of 22 are clean").
- **The 22-row table is a drill-down, never the front door.** *"Don't open me on a spreadsheet — that's the thing I'm paying you to pre-digest."*
- **Export:** owner/board one-pager.

**The fork, resolved explicitly:** the dentist wants calls-first; the VC wants efficiency-first. They do **not** need different layouts — they need the **same slot filled at a different altitude**. Slot 1 is always "the verdict"; for the dentist it's a call count, for the VC it's a CAC delta. Slot 3 is always "the money frame"; for the dentist it's dollars-of-production vs. retainer, for the VC it's CAC-vs-paid. The layout is one spine with a **per-segment outcome dictionary** and **two optional inserts** (competitor/authority block for B2B+services; portfolio triage for multi-location). One product, configured — not five products.

## D. The money mechanics

### What drives PAY-MORE / expand (map each to a UI moment)

| Expansion lever | UI moment | Personas |
|---|---|---|
| **Call/lead tracking + recording + quality scoring** | "You missed 6 callable new patients last month" surfaced as a flagged, dollarized loss next to the lead count | Dentist ("pure lost revenue — I'll pay to fix it immediately"), HVAC, churned ("hear the actual leads, judge quality myself") |
| **Closed-loop revenue attribution** (lead → CRM opportunity → closed-won) | The money frame (slot 3) graduates from "pipeline $" to "influenced revenue $" with a small "expand attribution" CTA | SaaS, VC, consulting, HVAC ("these 6 leads became $40K — I'll pay more and tell my friends") |
| **Do-more-of-the-work** (drafts, on-page, publishing end-to-end; partner-bylined content with a 10-min review) | Work-log items show "we can take this off your plate" affordance | SaaS ("I pay for capacity, not advice"), consulting ("partner time is the bottleneck — removing it is worth real money") |
| **Proactive recommendations with a projected $ impact** | A "next bets" card: "3 plays, here's what each could be worth" — never status-only | SaaS, VC ("a defensible forecast is worth more than any historical chart"), churned ("here's what one more $1k/mo buys") |
| **High-value service / market expansion** | "People in your area search for Invisalign/implants and you're not showing up" as a discrete opportunity card | Dentist (big-ticket services), multi-location (laggard-location turnaround, onboarding location #23 in two clicks) |
| **Competitive intelligence as a standing service** | The competitor block (B2B/services insert) offers "here's where Competitor X beats you + the content to close it" | SaaS, consulting, churned, multi-location ("benchmark my Westside against similar markets") |
| **AI-answer visibility** | New section: "do you show up when buyers ask ChatGPT 'who's best for X'" | SaaS, consulting ("no one else is selling me this yet — I'll pay a premium") |
| **The forwardable export** | A "download board-ready one-pager" button on the verdict | SaaS, VC, consulting, multi-location (all said saving the slide-rebuild tax is "real money") |

**The clearest single lever across all seven: make the upsell a transparent ROI math problem, not a sales pitch** (churned client's exact phrasing — echoed by the dentist, HVAC, SaaS, VC). Every expand moment must show the dollars in and the expected dollars out.

### What prevents CHURN / distrust — the trust guards

| Trust guard | Why / persona |
|---|---|
| **Numbers must reconcile with the client's own reality** (front desk, GA4/HubSpot, phone/inbox, clinic managers) | The #1 churn trigger, named by **all seven.** *"If the dashboard says 47 leads and my phone says 12, I'm gone that day and I'll assume everything else is inflated too."* Reconcile to the client's source of truth; if numbers can't match, explain why. |
| **Never show all-green while the underlying business is flat/broken** | *"A dashboard with no honest red is a marketing artifact, not a measurement tool"* (VC). *"With 22 locations, something is always wrong"* (multi-loc). The dentist: green arrows + empty chair makes it *worse.* |
| **Show honest dips and explain them** | Churned, HVAC, VC, multi-loc: *"I'd rather see a flat month with an honest plan than a curated highlight reel. Spin is the fastest way to lose me."* Honesty about bad months is what makes the wins believable. |
| **Don't count garbage as wins** | Spam/robocalls/existing-patient reschedules (dentist), job-seekers/students/competitor snoops (consulting), out-of-area tire-kickers (HVAC). Inflated lead counts = fastest distrust. |
| **Show consistent, visible attribution methodology** | VC: silent restatement of "organic-sourced" between quarters kills trust instantly. Multi-loc: "last month said 140, this month the same period says 95 — silent restatements destroy trust faster than a bad number." Lock the methodology; surface it. |
| **No jargon walls** | All seven. Jargon now reads as *deliberate obfuscation* to the burned clients. *"If I feel dumb looking at my own dashboard, I assume that's on purpose."* |
| **No stale data** | All seven. *"Last updated 3 weeks ago"* = "you stopped working but kept billing." |
| **Verifiable, dated, clickable work-log** | Churned: the literal reason he fired the last agency was *"never knowing what I was paying for."* Links to the live artifact are the trust core. |
| **No invented composite score the agency controls** | Churned + VC: a single number with no shown formula is "a fabrication risk and an instant trust kill." |
| **Easy data export / no lock-in feel** | Churned: *"if I can't export my own data, I'll read that as a hostage situation."* |
| **Protect credibility (services)** | Consulting would churn over one embarrassing partner-bylined piece. Quality gate before publish. |

## E. Anti-features — the noise to cut (cross-persona)

These were named as noise by **5-7 of the seven personas each.** Cut or hide them.

- **Keyword ranking tables / "you rank for 4,200 keywords" / position 1-100 grids.** Named noise by all seven. Replace with: map-pack position (local) or topic-vs-competitor authority (B2B/services); otherwise bury under "under the hood."
- **Raw impressions, sessions, users, pageviews as headline numbers.** All seven. *"I can't spend an impression."* Traffic is the metric the burned clients **trust least** because "I've watched traffic go up while my phone stayed quiet."
- **Domain Authority / Domain Rating / backlink counts / spam score.** All seven. *"Pure agency-speak,"* "I don't report these upward."
- **Invented composite "SEO health score" / "visibility score 82" gauges.** Six of seven, and an active *distrust* trigger for the VC and churned client — "a number you control with no formula."
- **Technical-audit checklists, Core Web Vitals, crawl stats, schema/meta/alt-text status as a primary view.** All seven. "That's your kitchen, not my dining room."
- **Bounce rate, time-on-page, pages-per-session, CTR.** All seven. "Internal plumbing."
- **Activity theater framed as results** — "we published 12 posts / fixed 40 issues / optimized 60 pages." VC: *"Effort is not outcome. Don't show me motion and call it progress."* (Note: the *work-log still exists* — but outcome-tagged and below results, never as the headline.)
- **Confetti, gamification, badges, speedometers, decorative gauges, "congrats!" animations.** Dentist ("I'm a 47-year-old dentist, not playing a game — reads as fluff covering thin results"), VC, churned. **Density over decoration.**
- **Cumulative all-time vanity counters** ("1,204 backlinks built!"). Churned: "manufacture progress without showing recent direction."
- **Generic industry news/tips widgets and AI-sounding "insights" that restate the obvious.** Consulting ("I have a feed"), churned ("if the summary reads like it was generated to fill space, cut it").
- **Social follower/like counts** unless tied to actual buyers.
- **Cherry-picked / shifting date windows.** HVAC + churned: a comparison window that quietly moves to flatter the chart is *manipulation* — lock the window.
- **(Multi-location only) opening the user on a 22-row spreadsheet.** "That's the thing I'm paying you to pre-digest."

## F. The return-hook design

**The shared truth, stated by all seven verbatim:** *"Left to my own devices, I'd open it maybe once a month — when the invoice hits."* **Do not design for habitual logins. Nobody at any segment will build the habit.** The dashboard is **where you land from a notification**, not a destination you remember to visit.

**The cadence problem and its solution differ only in trigger and channel:**

| Segment | Cadence | The 1-2 hooks that work |
|---|---|---|
| **Local SMB** | Once/month at invoice time | **(1) SMS/email alert tied to MY money or MY customers:** "New 2-star review — tap to respond," "You missed 4 new-patient calls this week," "Leads up 35%, best month yet — 2-min recap." **(2) A monthly plain-English recap pushed to the phone** (the dentist would pay for a 60-sec Loom). Never "your weekly report is ready" — *"I'll mute that in a week."* |
| **B2B/SaaS** | Monthly/biweekly at review/QBR prep | **(1) A push with the one ROI line + "X items need your approval" (one-click to clear).** **(2) A real win worth forwarding** ("this piece just drove 12 demos") or a deadline'd blocker. |
| **Board/VC** | Quarterly at board prep | **A two-line push: "Organic CAC $X, down Y% QoQ, now Z% below paid; payback N months."** Good news → they click to verify and brag; bad news → they click to interrogate. The dashboard is "where I go to check the math." |
| **Professional services** | Monthly at partner update | **(1) Event nudge:** "new qualified inquiry from [topic]," "competitor overtook you on a deal-driving term," "partner piece waiting for your 5-min review." **(2) The forwardable partner-ready monthly summary** — "that single feature would make me log in every month on purpose." |
| **Multi-location** | Monthly at owner check-in (weekly if pushed well) | **A location-specific alert that deep-links straight to the location:** "Westside profile suspended — costing ~15 calls/week," "Northgate up 40%, here's why." *"If it lands me exactly on it, I'll open it weekly."* |

**The two universal mechanics that solve cadence for everyone:**
1. **Event-driven push tied to the client's money, customers, or a decision blocked on them** — never a "report is ready" nag. Every notification must be something they'd genuinely want to know as a business owner. *"Earn the open; don't beg for it."*
2. **A forwardable, zero-edit summary** (text/email for local; one-pager for B2B/board/services/multi-loc). For the upward-reporting personas this *is* the return hook — they come back to grab the thing they forward.

---

## G. The non-negotiables (without these, it fails)

1. **The dollarized/outcome-ized verdict vs. spend, dated against a baseline — readable in under 60 seconds, in the client's own words.** *"You got me X customers worth ~$Y; you cost me $Z."* Named by every persona as **the product itself** — "everything else is supporting evidence" / "decoration." This is the one thing they would never cancel.

2. **Reconciliation with the client's own reality.** The numbers must match the front desk, the inbox, HubSpot/GA4, the clinic managers. **One unexplained discrepancy and trust in the entire instrument collapses** — the single most-cited churn trigger across all seven.

3. **The "what needs ME" decision queue (or an explicit "nothing needed").** Without it, work silently stalls on the client's side and they blame the agency for slow results. All seven demanded it by name; it also doubles as the strongest return hook.

4. **Verifiable proof-of-work: a dated, outcome-tagged, clickable work-log sitting below results.** The thing that converts "are you actually working?" suspicion into trust — the literal reason the churned client fired his last agency, and the reassurance every owner-operator asked for.

5. **Honest measurement: no all-green, visible attribution methodology, no invented black-box scores, no stale data, no jargon.** The dashboard is a **trust instrument**; the moment it feels like spin — curated highlights, hidden dips, a composite score the agency controls — it stops being believed, and a disbelieved dashboard is worse than none because it actively signals you're hiding empty chairs.

# Part 2 — Gap Analysis: their spec vs. what we built

## 1. Verdict

We built our spec, not theirs — and the gap is structural, not cosmetic. The clients asked for a **trust instrument**: a one-line dollarized verdict against a baseline, an outcome count in real human units (calls, leads, qualified inquiries) clickable to named records, a money frame that puts customer value next to the retainer, a "what needs me" queue, a verifiable work-log, and everything else buried — all reconciling to the client's own reality. What we shipped is a beautifully disciplined **content-curation cockpit**: the hero is "Your content plan," the verdict is a dateless visibility-band sentence with no number a client can check, and the entire outcome/money/reconciliation spine is either absent or actively forbidden by design ("CANNOT show: booked revenue, leads/close-rate/LTV, any time-over-time trend/delta"). We got the *anti-features* almost perfectly right (no keyword tables, no vanity scores up top, no jargon, no confetti, honest "estimate" labeling, no booked-revenue overclaim) and we got the *spine ordering* exactly backwards from what all seven personas dictated: we lead with the plan (slot 5 in their spine) and bury the proof (slots 1–3) behind a `<details>` toggle. The single most-cited non-negotiable across all seven personas — **the dollarized outcome-vs-spend verdict, dated against a baseline** — is not just missing, it is explicitly prohibited by the evergreen contract we locked. We built a sophisticated product the personas did not order, on top of a chassis (recommendations engine) we already had, and we declared the thing they all called "the product itself" out of scope.

## 2. Already aligned (KEEP)

Give credit where it's due — several things match the spec's *spirit* tightly:

- **Anti-feature discipline is excellent.** No keyword ranking tables up top, no DA/DR/backlink counts, no invented composite "health score" gauge as the headline, no confetti/gamification, no raw impressions-as-headline. The CompactStatBar (impressions/clicks/position) is correctly buried behind "See full report." This is exactly what 5–7 personas demanded in Section E.
- **The "what needs me" queue exists and leads.** `ActionQueueStrip` is slot 1, renders `null` when empty (the "nothing needed, close the tab" state the personas wanted), and is suppressed in preview. This is non-negotiable #3, and we honored both the presence and the empty-state law.
- **Honest money framing on the ROI surface.** `ROIDashboard` in evergreen mode is genuinely disciplined: "directional traffic-value model, not a promise of booked revenue," "We do not multiply by lead value, close rate, or lifetime value," explicit methodology disclosure. This directly answers the VC's "false precision reads as spin" and the churned client's "no black-box score" guards.
- **Verifiable work-in-flight + loop status.** `IssueLoopFooter` shows "greenlit N · M in discussion · N briefs in progress" — a real, non-theatrical proof-of-motion that maps to non-negotiable #4's intent (proof of work), and `WinsSurface` is outcome-tagged ("Published new post," "Won local pack visibility") rather than a task-log dump.
- **No jargon, no purple, narrative copy.** The evergreen copy contract, the client-friendly "Also on your plan" labels ("Protecting your rankings," "Quick wins"), and the absence of archetype/severity/confidence labels all satisfy the "no jargon walls" guard.
- **The human-curation moat ("Curated by your strategist") is a genuine trust signal** that no persona named but that supports the "is a real person doing this work?" anxiety underneath the work-log demand.

## 3. Diverges (CHANGE)

Where we built something related but in a shape the clients did not ask for:

- **The verdict is a band sentence, not a checkable number.**
  *What we have:* `NarratedStatusHeadline` → "Your search visibility is building, with real room to grow" + a visibility-score ring. Keyed off a 0–100 band, no baseline, no delta, no dollars, no outcome count.
  *What they want:* "New patient inquiries this month: 14, up from 9 last month" / "Organic CAC $X, down Y% vs last Q." A *conclusion with a number the client can check against their own reality*, dated against a baseline.
  *The change:* Replace (or precede) the band sentence with a real outcome-or-dollar verdict against a baseline. The visibility score is precisely the "invented composite score the agency controls with no dollar translation" the VC and churned client said is an *instant trust kill* — it must not be the headline number.

- **The spine is inverted: plan leads, proof is hidden.**
  *What we have:* Order is Your turn → status → **Content plan (HERO)** → Also on plan → **Proof band collapsed behind `<details>`** → Wins → Competitors → Ask.
  *What they want:* verdict → outcome count → money frame → what-needs-me → work-log → everything-SEO buried. Proof is slots 1–3; the content plan ("what's shipping next") is slot 5.
  *The change:* Promote the proof band out of the collapsed `<details>` and above the content plan. "Activity/plan is reassurance, not the headline" was said verbatim by the HVAC owner; we made the plan the headline and the proof a click away.

- **"Evergreen / no time-over-time" contradicts the most-repeated requirement.**
  *What we have:* A locked contract banning "this week," "vs last refresh," dates, and *all* deltas. Proof is "current state, never time-relative."
  *What they want:* "a number with no baseline is just a number I can't judge" (HVAC + churned, verbatim). The verdict *must* be dated against a baseline ("vs. when we started: 47 vs 31"). Trend-vs-last-period AND vs-baseline was demanded by all seven for slot 2.
  *The change:* The evergreen stance is the single biggest self-inflicted divergence. It needs to be relaxed to allow at least one anchored comparison ("vs. when we started"). Evergreen *framing* is fine for the plan; an evergreen *verdict with no baseline* fails the explicit client demand.

- **Money frame is "cost-to-buy traffic value," not "customer value vs retainer."**
  *What we have:* ROI dashboard = organic traffic value / ad-spend equivalent.
  *What they want:* "14 new patients ≈ $11,000 in new production. Your retainer: $1,500" (local) or "pipeline $ → ratio 4.2x / CAC vs paid" (B2B/board).
  *The change:* Traffic-value is a defensible *secondary* metric, but it is not the money frame the personas asked for. The frame must put *outcome value next to spend*. Even a clearly-labeled estimate ("~$X in estimated new business vs. your $Y retainer") is what they wanted; cost-to-buy-traffic is an SEO-native proxy they did not ask for.

- **Competitor block is Premium-gated for everyone; should be segment-conditional.**
  *What we have:* `CompetitorGapsSection` shown to all, gated to Premium with a placeholder.
  *What they want:* Competitor/authority is a *B2B + professional-services insert*; local SMB explicitly wants *no competitor table*. Altitude, not tier, should decide whether it appears.

## 4. Missing (BUILD)

Ranked by how many personas demanded it:

1. **Reconciliation to the client's own reality — calls/leads/inquiries in real human units, clickable to the actual named records. (7/7 personas, the #1 churn trigger.)** We show clicks/impressions/visitors/conversions-count. We do NOT show "14 new patient inquiries" or "9 qualified inbound" clickable to a named list. This is the load-bearing trust mechanic and it is absent. The "CANNOT show: leads/close-rate" line is the spec violation.

2. **The dollarized outcome-vs-spend verdict, dated against a baseline. (7/7 — "the product itself.")** Missing and prohibited by design. See §3.

3. **Call/lead tracking + recording + quality scoring (the clearest pay-more lever). (Dentist, HVAC, churned — 3+, strongest local upsell.)** "You missed 6 callable new patients" as a dollarized flagged loss. Nothing in the build.
