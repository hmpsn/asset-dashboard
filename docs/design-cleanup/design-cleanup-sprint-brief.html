The four through-lines

Across six screens the same four problems recur. Naming them is how 30 findings become one coherent body of work rather than a to-do pile — each wave below advances one or more of these.

1 · Rank what matters
The data layer is excellent, so every signal earns equal weight — and nothing leads. Cap hero metrics at three; promote the worklist; demote the rest. (CC-F2/F3, WH-F1/F6, ISSUE-F1, AC-F1)
2 · One model, said once
Multiple navigations / representations describe the same thing differently. One nav, one health widget, one journey. (CP-F1, CD-F1, WH-F2, CD-F2)
3 · Reuse the system, don't re-roll it
Tab bars, menus, gradient cards, disclosures, section headers, attention rows are hand-rolled per screen. Lift them into shared primitives. (CP-F2/F5, AC-F2, ISSUE-F4/F5, WH-F5)
4 · Color means status, never chrome
Mint on data, purple on a client metric, amber on normal counts, raw hex instead of tokens. One sweep enforces the four laws + tokenizes every warning surface. (CC-F5, WH-F3, CP-F4, AC-F3, CD-F5)

03

Suggested schedule

Two tracks run in parallel: a design-system track (Waves 0 + 0b) and a product track (the screen waves), with the screen waves pulling from the foundation as it lands.

Waves 0 + 0b · foundations→W1 Command Center→W2 Workspace Home→W3 The Issue→W4 Pipeline→W5 Client shell
Sequencing logic: P1s (Command Center header + worklist) ship in Wave 1 for an early visible win. The design-system track front-loads so no screen wave hand-rolls a component twice. The color/token sweep (0b) can land independently at any point — it's the safest, broadest improvement. Wave 5 is the heaviest and most isolated, so it can run on its own track without blocking the others.
04

Appendix — future audits & design strategy

Captured so nothing is lost — to revisit after this sprint. Two buckets: surfaces/flows not yet audited, and higher-level moves that would make the product more powerful, not just more consistent.

Audit gaps — by leverage
Onboarding / activation, end-to-end
The full new-workspace journey (login → connect site → GSC/GA4 → first audit → first brief). Where retention is won; only seen in pieces so far.
Empty & zero-data states, systematically
A day-one workspace has no rankings, traffic, or audit. How every surface degrades when empty is make-or-break for first impressions.
Data-viz consistency
This is a charts product — the charts are the value. Audit whether the styleguide's deep data-viz tokens are applied consistently (axis discipline, annotations, "what changed & why" overlay). Your clearest differentiator.
Off-platform brand surfaces
The weekly digest email + the forwardable one-pager (IssueExportBar, brief-export-html) — where the client's boss sees hmpsn. Usually neglected; pure brand ambassadors.
AI advisor loop · light theme · mobile
The chat/"ask your strategist" UX; full light-theme parity (almost always an afterthought); and the mobile story behind MobileGuard.
Strategic moves — power, not just polish
Add a non-color cue to the four laws
Meaning is encoded almost entirely in color; ~8% of men are colorblind and color-only status fails them. Pair each law with a shape/icon/weight cue — an accessibility win and a more robust system.
Propagate the "verdict-first" pattern
The Issue client page's lead-with-the-dollar, narrated approach is the best design idea in the app. Most other surfaces still lead with raw data. Spreading it is a product strategy.
Formalize density modes & a motion system
A "comfortable vs compact" density token serves operators (dense) and clients (calm) from one system. And codify when things animate (ScannerReveal, ring charge-up, stagger) so it feels premium, not incidental.
