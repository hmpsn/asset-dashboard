UX Review · client dashboard shell

The client shell
is mid-migration.

A review of ClientDashboard — the outer client navigation that wraps every client surface (including The Issue). It's clearly in transit: a client-ia-v2 flag collapses a sprawling ~12-tab nav into a tight 4-tab "two-speed" shell. The two-speed model is the right destination. The findings are about finishing that move — because today both models coexist, and the same surfaces are mounted in two places at once.

What's already right

The findings

CD-F1

Two navigation models ship side by side

High · IA

Behind client-ia-v2 the nav collapses from ~12 top-level tabs (Overview, Performance, Health, Strategy, Inbox, Content Plan, Plans, ROI, Brand, Results, Deep Dive, Settings) to a focused four. Twelve tabs is far too many for a client who mostly wants "is my money working?" — and while the flag is mid-rollout, which nav a given client sees depends on flag state, so the product has two front doors.

Now — legacy: 12 tabs
OverviewPerformanceHealthStrategyInboxContent PlanPlansROIBrandResultsDeep DiveSettings
choice overload; unclear where "my results" live
Fix — two-speed: 4 tabs
OverviewDeep DiveInboxSettings
headline first; depth + admin folded in

RecommendationCommit to the two-speed shell and retire the legacy nav. Overview is the verdict; Deep Dive holds Performance/Health/Rankings/Content as sub-views; Inbox is the loop; Settings absorbs Plans/Brand. Finishing the client-ia-v2 migration is the single highest-impact move on this screen.

CD-F2

The same surfaces are mounted in two places

Medium · duplication

Health, Performance, Strategy, Content Plan, Brand, and Plans each render both as a standalone tab and as a slot inside Deep Dive or Settings — with the full prop list duplicated inline (the HealthTab + its actionPlanSlot is repeated verbatim). Two routes reach identical content, and the two call sites can silently drift (one gets a prop the other doesn't).

Now — mounted twice
HealthTab — as /health tabmount A
HealthTab — in Deep Dive healthSlotmount B (verbatim)
PerformanceTab · StrategyTab · …also ×2
Fix — define once, place once
const healthPanel = <HealthTab …/>one def
Deep Dive › Health → healthPanelone home

RecommendationDefine each panel once (a const or a small registry keyed by tab id) and reference it wherever it appears — no inline duplication. And decide each surface's single home: under the two-speed shell, Health/Performance/Strategy live inside Deep Dive, not also as top-level tabs. One definition, one route.

CD-F3

Up to three notices stack above every tab

Medium · density

Above the tab content sit, in sequence: a trial-countdown banner (or the trial-ended banner), a section-errors InlineBanner, and a per-tab SeoEducationTip. On a trial workspace with a transient query error, the client meets three stacked interruptions before the actual page — the same "wall before the work" seen on the Content Pipeline (CP-F3).

RecommendationGive the shell one notice region with a priority order (errors > trial > education tip) that shows at most one at a time, or docks them compactly. The education tip especially should yield to a real error. Reuse whatever consolidation pattern lands for CP-F3.

CD-F4

The per-tab page title just repeats the nav label

Low · redundancy

Every tab renders a PageHeader whose title is the active tab's label — click "Health," the page says "Health." The nav already shows where you are, so the H1 echoes it without adding information; only the subtitle (workspace name, last-scan date) carries something new.

RecommendationEither drop the redundant title echo (let the subtitle + content carry the page) or make the header earn its space — a verdict line, a freshness stamp, the primary action for that tab. A title that only repeats the nav is a wasted row at the top of every screen.

CD-F5

Trial banners hardcode amber/red instead of warning tokens

Low · token drift

The trial-countdown and trial-ended banners use raw bg-amber-500/8 border-amber-500/20 and bg-red-500/8 border-red-500/20 mixed with the text-accent-warning/danger token classes — a half-tokenized warning surface, and a third spelling of "amber notice" alongside InlineBanner and the cockpit's staleness nudge (AC-F3).

RecommendationRoute both banners through the InlineBanner component (or its token-backed warning/danger variants) so every alert surface in the app shares one definition. Folds into the tokenize-hardcoded-hex theme already in the sprint.

Read in context

This screen isn't broken — it's between two designs, and the new one (the two-speed shell) is right. The most valuable thing here is to finish the migration (CD-F1) and collapse the duplicate mounts (CD-F2); the rest (notice stacking, title echo, token drift) are shared themes already captured for other screens. That completes the five-screen review program.
